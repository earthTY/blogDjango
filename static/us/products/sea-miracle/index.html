Causes of hair loss and weaken hair

The most common provoking factors that lead to loss and lifelessness of hair include:

  • deficiency of vitamins and microelements;
  • frequent staining of hair;
  • external adverse factors;
  • thermal impact;
  • frequent stress.

Hormonal changes are characteristic during the period of childbirth, menopause, and also in the presence of internal pathologies. This, in turn, leads to a decrease in the protective functions of the body and the weakening of all vital internal systems. The person is disturbed by blood circulation, in connection with which the hair begins to not receive nutrients.

Staining, chemical perm and frequent use of a hair dryer lead to thinning of the hair shaft, weakening of the hair bulb and, as a consequence, to hair loss.

Negative impact on the appearance of strands can also external factors such as ultraviolet. Sunlight deprives hair's natural moisture, as a result, the hair will become dull and brittle.

Frequent stresses lead to the release of the hormone cortisol, which adversely affects the entire body, including the health of the hair.
